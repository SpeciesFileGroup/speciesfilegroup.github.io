Current and past events hosted by the Species File Group

Questions? Chat with us on Gitter or contact our community liaison Deborah Paul.

Current

To join any of the Zoom meetings you will need a link to the Zoom room. There are two ways to get the link:

  • Be sent the Zoom link via email as a reminder before the meeting (typically 1-2 days before). To do so, a) subscribe to this email list: https://lists.illinois.edu/lists/info/sfg-announce and b) click "confirm" in the first email you receive from the listserv.
  • Chat with a member of the Species File Group and its collaborators at https://gitter.im/SpeciesFileGroup/taxonworks.
We do not post the links here to prevent Zoom bombing.

Weekly Digitization and TaxonWorks

  • What: Help grow a community of digitization expertise around TaxonWorks. A 1-2 hr weekly open question and answer session with a focus on natural history specimen digitization using TaxonWorks. The agenda is set by those with specific questions. Updates to TaxonWorks are briefly covered. Design and discussion of new functionality frequently happens.
  • When: Wednesdays, weekly. 13:30 CST (19:30 UTC),
  • Where: Zoom.
  • Who: Open to anyone.
  • Add to calendar (ICS file).

Nomenclature and TaxonWorks

  • What: TaxonWorks excels at digitizing nomenclature and its metadata. The agenda is set by users with specific questions and use cases. In addition we highlight new functionality related to digitization of nomenclature.
  • When: Wednesdays, alternating other week (currently ). 10:30 CST (16:30 UTC),
  • Where: Zoom.
  • Who: Open to anyone.
  • Add to calendar (ICS file).

Guided exploration of nomenclature in TaxonWorks

  • What: A somewhat simpler, more guided exploration of nomenclature (and related data) handling in TaxonWorks. Sessions are often class-like for part or most of the time. Downstream sessions are planned with collaborators according to their needs and the general discussion that occurs in these meetings.
  • When: Wednesdays, alternating other week (currently ). 10:30 CDT (15:30 UTC),
  • Where: Zoom.
  • Who: Lead and taught by Jim Woolley and colloaborators. Open to anyone.
  • Add to calendar (ICS file).
